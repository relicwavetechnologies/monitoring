The Problem

Visa rules, fees, and appointment availability change without warning. The only way to stay current today is to manually open each portal and remember what it used to say.

  • Time-consuming — Someone has to open five different websites every morning, read through them, and compare against memory
  • Slow to notice — A fee change published Monday might not be spotted until Friday — meaning days of incorrect advice to clients
  • No history — When something does change, there's no record of what it said before, or exactly when it changed
  • Can't scale — Five sites today. Twenty sites tomorrow. Manual checking collapses under any growth
  • Alert fatigue — Simple "notify me of any change" tools fire hundreds of alerts a day for trivial things like timestamps, cookie banners, and button colour tweaks
What VisaWatch does instead

One dashboard showing all sites. An email in your inbox only when something actually matters — with an AI-written summary of exactly what changed and why it's important. A full history of every change ever detected, with the exact before-and-after text. No manual checking. No noise.

03

How It Works

Every site is checked once a day. Most checks find nothing new and finish silently in seconds. When something does change, VisaWatch puts it through several layers of filtering before deciding whether it's worth telling you about.

What happens on each daily check
01
Fetch the page
VisaWatch loads the site — waiting for JavaScript to fully render if needed — then zooms in on only the section that contains policy or fee content, ignoring headers, footers, ads, and navigation.
02
Compare to yesterday
The cleaned text is fingerprinted and compared to the last saved version. If nothing changed — which is true on most days — the check ends here. No noise, no processing cost.
03
Filter out the noise
If something looks different, two more checks run: is the change big enough to matter (tiny wording tweaks are ignored), and does it show up on two consecutive days (one-day glitches disappear on their own)? Only changes that pass both make it through.
04
AI reads and summarises
A language model reads the changed text — not the raw code, just the words — and writes a plain-English summary: what changed, what category it falls into (fee, policy, appointment, etc.), and how important it is on a scale of 1–5.
05
Important change (score 3, 4 or 5)
Email + Dashboard
An alert email lands in your inbox with the AI summary and the exact text that changed. The change is also saved to the dashboard for the full history.
Minor change (score 1 or 2)
Dashboard only
Logged and visible in the dashboard for reference. No email — it's not worth interrupting you for.

05

The Dashboard

A private, login-protected web app where you can see everything VisaWatch has ever detected — across all sites, going back as far as the tool has been running.

Overview page

  • At-a-glance stats — total sites, total changes found, and how many were significant enough to alert on
  • Important changes feed — every notable change in reverse order, with a one-line AI summary, change category, and colour-coded severity
  • Minor changes — lower-importance changes are listed separately so nothing is hidden, but they don't clutter the main feed
  • Send Digest — one button in Settings to email yourself a summary of the top recent changes on demand

Per-site page

  • Full history — every change ever detected for that site, in chronological order, with timestamps
  • Before & after — click any change to see exactly which words were added (green) and which were removed (red)
  • Site health — last checked time, total snapshots taken, and whether monitoring is active or paused
  • Site config — see the monitoring settings for each site; pause or trigger a manual check without touching code

06

Email Alerts

When something important is detected, an email lands in your inbox — no login required to understand what changed. Two ways to receive updates.

Automatic alert
Fires when it matters
Sent the moment a significant change is confirmed. You don't have to check anything — VisaWatch finds you.
On-demand digest
Send when you need it
Hit "Send Digest" in Settings to get a summary of the top recent notable changes emailed to you immediately — useful for briefings or sharing with colleagues.
Every alert email includes
  • Clear subject — e.g. "[Important] VFS UAE→SGP: Application fee raised to $185 effective May 1" — you know what it's about before you open it
  • Plain-English summary — written by the AI, not a raw technical diff. One sentence that tells you what changed and why it matters
  • The exact change — the words that were added (highlighted green) and removed (highlighted red), limited to what's relevant
  • One-click link — straight into the dashboard to see the full detail, history, and context
Minor changes stay off your radar

Only changes scored 3 or above (out of 5) trigger an email. Cosmetic tweaks, typo corrections, and reordering of existing content are stored silently in the dashboard for reference but will never interrupt you.

Built With

Straightforward modern stack — all managed services, no infrastructure to maintain.

Dashboard
Next.js · Vercel
The web app you see and log into. Hosted on Vercel — no servers to manage, deploys automatically when code is updated.
Database
Postgres · Neon
Stores every change ever detected, every snapshot ever taken, and all site configuration. Serverless — scales to zero when idle.
AI
GPT-5.4-mini
Reads changed text and writes the plain-English summaries you see in emails and on the dashboard. Only called when a real change passes all filters — keeps costs near zero.
Email
Resend
Delivers alert emails and digest emails. Also handles the magic-link login so there's no password to forget.
